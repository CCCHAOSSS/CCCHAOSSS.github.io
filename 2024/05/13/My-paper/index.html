Dear experts and scholars, My name is LLM from zjut in China and I am here to talk about my paper, which was accepted at ijcnn2024, named as “An Event-based Feature Representation Method for Event Stream Classification using Deep Spiking Neural Networks “
First of all, I would like to introduce the research background of this paper.

Spiking neural networks, or SNN for short, are a type of artificial neural network that more closely simulates the behavior of biological neurons in the brain，pic(a) and (b) show how Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) and SNN neurons operate, respectively.
Unlike ANN, which operate using continuous activation values, SNNs communicate through discrete, asynchronous spikes. The neurons of SNN can encode information using the timing of spikes. SNN typically have lower computational and energy consumption. The learning rules for SNNs include the usual gradient descent, backpropagation, and timing-related rules, such as STDP.

Dynamic Vision Sensors are a new type of imaging sensors which directly encode relative brightness changes in scene /siːn/ and output asynchronous /eɪˈsɪŋkrənəs/ event streams in the form of quadruples /kwɑːˈdruːplz/, including the time stamp, two-dimensional coordinates and polarity /pəˈlærəti/ of each event. SNN is naturally suited for processing this temporal information since their neurons can encode information using the timing of spikes. However, integrating event streams directly into deep SNNs poses challenges.
Now this is the most used method in deep SNN which accumulates events into frames before training. First, an event stream sample is divided into fixed segments in the time dimension, which we generally refer to as the time step T of the network, and then it is accumulated into different channels according to the positive and negative polarity of the events, so that a range of static images can be obtained, like this pics shown.

AND There are two methods commonly used for dividing events into T time steps, which are quantitative-based method and time-based method. quantitative-based method is to divide the total number of events evenly into each time step, and time-based method is to choose a time interval delatT to intercept the event stream samples.
The choice of parameters T and deltat are usually based on experience, resulting in numerous manual attempts, poor generalization and robustness, particularly when dealing with the datasets of different speeds.

Event-based models are known for their ability to accurately utilize temporal information from event streams during feature extraction.
In this work, we introduced event-based feature extraction method based into deep SNNs, and proposed a novel feature descriptor called time interval correlation time-surface, or TICTS for short.
In event-based models, Time-Surface (TS) is a useful feature descriptor, which describes the temporal context of an event within a certain space and the exponentially /ˌɛkspoʊˈnɛnʃəli/ decaying effects of historical events on current event.

These are two common time-surface. HOTS used recent historical events to construct the time-surface, resulting in the time-surface susceptible to noise. HATS utilized multiple historical events within a fixed time window to compute, aiming to reduce noise sensitivity. However, it increased computational time and algorithmic complexity since the normalization. Here a time surface is visualized, Event-based feature extraction is performed on such time-surfaces.
In order to better address the noise problem while maintaining the exponential decay of the time-surface and avoiding normalization operations, we proposed time interval correlation time-surface (TICTS) .The calculations are shown in this figure. We chose to use the average historical time of the last two historical events to calculate the time surface, which avoids noise interference to some extent. At the same time, in order to avoid too much loss of the time sensitivity, we add a correction factor chi to this.

Here is the entire training framework for our model. We perform event stream data feature extraction based on the proposed TICTS first. Then the obtained feature events are converted into frames for the deep SNN.

The training of the SNN is based on the STBP. This is a friendly gradient descent training method and does not require any complex skills. You can easily find the instructions online, so I won’t go into too much detail here.

We experimented with both methods of dividing events in the conversion mentioned earlier. Datasets are DVS128Gesture DailyAction and MNIST-DVS. More details are shown in this PowerPoint and Table 1.
The first experiment /ɪkˈsperɪmənt/ is to verify the validity /vəˈlɪdəti/ of our proposed TICTS on a purely event-based model. Compared with HOTS and HATS, the accuracy has been improved on both datasets by only changing the feature descriptor to TICTS. The improved results indicate that our proposed descriptor TICTS is effective.

While we are not very concerned about the accuracy on event-based model, as we only obtain feature events by one layer of event-based method based on TICTS in our SNNs.

The second experiment/ɪkˈsperɪmənt/ is based on Different Speeds Datasets. We converted feature events into frames and feed them into frame-driven SNN models.

In practical tasks, the speed of event streams datasets usually varies depending on the recording environment and objects. The faster objects cause faster change in light intensity, and DVS asynchronously /eɪˈsɪŋkrənəs/ records these events, resulting in difficult to distinguish the resulting frames. This pic visualizes an event stream sample called “right arm counter clockwise” in DVS128Gesture datasets with different speeds and same detat.
It can be seen that the slower the speed, the less the number of events contained in a frame obtained at the same time interval detat, and a complete object trajectory [trəˈdʒektəri] cannot be represented. The faster the speed, the events are overlapped and there are much redundant information.

In our model, we used a layer of event-based feature extraction with TICTS to preprocess datasets, then like other works, converted the obtained events into frames by events accumulation. For clarity, in the following content we will simply refer to our method with TICTS as E-F method, and refer to the method of directly converting the original event streams into frames without event-based preprocessing as (STD) method.

The following two graphs show the results on the two datasets, results of both methods were obtained under the same networks structure and parameters. We can see our results are mostly more accurate than those of STD and have stronger stability on datasets with different speeds.

The third experiment is the results on DVS128Gesture at different speeds by quantitative-based division for event streams. It can be seen that as T increases, the accuracy of STD also increases, which is intuitive because more time steps can enable SNN to better capture continuous time movements and changes. However, this increases is limited. Our method maintains good accuracy at less time steps and exhibits stable adaptability to changes in dataset speed.

Compared with other deep SNNs methods, our model is also competitive because we achieved a similar level of accuracy using a smaller network architecture. The results on DVS128Gesture and MNIST-DVS can be seen in the Table three. It should be noted that DailyAction has hardly been used in deep SNNs, so we have not listed it. Finally, Let me sum up our work.

Our primary contributions are summarized as follows:

  • we introduced event-based feature extraction into deep SNNs to construct a classification model for event streams. To the best of our knowledge, our model is the first one which combines event-based method with deep SNNs in a single model.

  • We constructing a new type Time Surface, which considered two historical events in the past. Consider not only the time difference between the historical event and the current event, but also the time difference between the historical events.

  • Our method can achieve higher accuracy than the standard method which converts the raw event streams into frames without the event-based processing. When facing different speed datasets, our method exhibits good robustness with reduced dependence on the time parameter

That is what our work is all about, Our study has preliminary /prɪˈlɪmɪneri/ evidence of the efficacy /ˈefɪkəsi/ of integrating event-based method and deep SNNs, potentially serving as a catalyst /ˈkætəlɪst/ for further scholarly /ˈskɑːlərli/ research.
Thank you for your listening.
